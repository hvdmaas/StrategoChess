Stratego Chess
Stratego Chess combines ideas from Stratego and Chess on a standard chess board with standard chess pieces. One pawn per player is secretly marked as the flag. The goal is not checkmate: you win by capturing the opponent's hidden flag pawn.

    Stratego Chess

    By Han van der Maas.

    Objective

    Capture the opponent's flag pawn.

    Rules (differences from chess)

    • The king can be captured; this does not end the game.
    • No check rule: you are not required to move out of check.
    • Capturing the flag pawn wins the game.
    • Stalemate does not apply. If the flag must move into check, it must. If a player has no legal moves, they lose.
    • Castling is not allowed.
    • En passant is allowed.
    • Other draw rules still apply (threefold repetition, 50‑move rule, insufficient material, draw by agreement).

    Setup

    Start from the normal chess position. Each player secretly chooses one of their pawns as the flag. When a pawn is captured, it is revealed whether it was the flag.
